Miróbriga Ruins

October 14, 2018
Heritage

My first time in Miróbriga Ruins was to make a presentation, with my colleague Alexandrino Gonçalves, related to the use of technologies among archaeological sites. Invited by the archaeologist José Carlos Quaresma, this presentation was tyargeted for students of archaeology. It was in 2017 and, back then, I was amazed by the cultural richness of this archaeological site.

Classified as a Site of Public Interest in 1940, the archaeological site at Miróbriga has seen under the Portuguese Architectural Heritage Institute since 1982.

The city

Considered by some investigators as a sanctuary and by others as a provincial urban centre, Miróbriga was inhabited from, at least, the Iron Age to the fourth century AD. It is believed that this land rich in agricultural, maritime, and mining resources, had great commercial importance. Miróbriga probably acquired the rank of municipality during the Flavian dinasty (1st century AD).

Mentioned since the sixteenth century by the humanist, André Resende, Miróbriga has been the scene of several excavations since the early nineteenth century, whilst conservation, restoration and maintainance works have been carried out throughout the present century.

Prior to Roman occupation and Iron Age settlement was found in the highest zone of Miróbriga by people of possible Celtic origin. Roman influence attains this region in the 2nd century A.D. that the city acquires most of its present urban characteristics.

The full extension of this site is not yet known. However, it presents some remarkable features, such as the forum, the residential and commercial zones, the bath complex, the bridge, the stone paved streets, and most of all, its circus, where bigae and quadrigae races took place drawn by Lusitanian horses, famous throughtout the Roman world.

The Forum

At the highest level of the urban area, a forum was built over the previous occupation. This public square was the centre round which the buildings constituting the poilitical, administrative, economic, and religious centre of the town took their place. Occupying an important position in the forum is a centralized temple dedicated to Venus.

The public square is surrounded by buildings about which little is known as yet, but which may be the buildings of the provincial fora - “cúria” and “basílica”.

To the south, was the commercial area with various shops, tabernae. These were usually small rooms that opened directly onto the street. In some cases they had a parcial upper story, mezzanine, where the shop owner lived, steps led from the street to the main square.

Ending Notes About Miróbriga

About Anabela Marto
Anabela Marto is an Invited Lecturer in the Department of Informatics Engineering at the Superior School of Technology and Management (ESTG) - Polytechnic of Leiria (IPLeiria) - and a Researcher at Computer Science and Communication Research Centre (CIIC). Her research topics are related to Mobile Augmented Reality applied in Cultural Heritage contexts. She is graduated in Computer Engineering (ESTG - IPLeiria), has a master degree in Multimedia - specialization in Technologies (FEUP - Faculty of Engineering of the University of Porto) and she is a PhD student in Informatics (UTAD - University of Trás-os-Montes and Alto Douro).
